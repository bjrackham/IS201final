Brinsen's Blog for Bike Trails

This blog is a space to post about some of my favorite bike trails. The idea came when I saw this tableus visual showing that cycling races were one of the least favorite activities across demographics. I've loved to ride for a long time and started racing to connect with community and have more fun. I'm hoping to raise that statistic by helping people find more places to go ride and get them excited about it by sharing some of my favorite trails!


----Trails----

 

Barrel Roll
Santa Clara, Utah
  • Length: 5.4 mile loop   
  • Elevation Gain: 547ft   
  • Difficulty: Moderate   

Barrel Roll is probably my favorite trail in Utah. It starts with a sustained climb while looking out into the canyon range below. This climb leads into a fast and flowy descent leading you into the second climb that features technical aspects to challenge you. The rest of the trails has more flowing descent that weaves through the canyons. The trail is full of fun features but has alternate routes for less expereienced riders. It's the perfect place to grow your skills while taking in beautiful views!

 
 
   
Jem
Virgin, Utah
  • Length: 5.4 miles
  • Elevation Gain: 547ft
  • Difficulty: Easy

Jem sits under the shadow of the Redbull Rampage course in Virgin, Utah. Jem is a common favorite known for its unbeatable flow. Enjoy riding at any pace with endless views of the dessert. It is a dirrectional trail, but if you want to turn it into a longer ride, Jem connects into a longer trail system with three other favorites

  1. Hurricane Rim
    • 6.4 miles
    • 553 vertical feet
  2. Dead Ringer
    • 5.2 miles
    • 578 vertical feet
  3. Cryptobionic
    • 1.3 miles
    • -159 vertical feet

Star Wars
Banff, Alberta
  • Length: 1.8 mile loop
  • Elevation Gain: 374ft
  • Difficulty: Advanced

Star Wars is one of the most unique trails I've ever rode. It weaves through a pine forest with unreal views of Banff's mountains. The loop itself is extremely easy and simple. The exciting part comes from the man made features. Trail builders have gone through to create wooden bridges, drops, jumps, and spirals. Their work has made this a one of a kind ride. Anyone can enjoy the ride, but to fully appreciate the features you should be comfortable in your technical skills.
